Energy Conservation Simulation
Description
This physics simulation demonstrates the conservation of mechanical energy. The initial potential energy of the system present in the spring is converted into the kinetic and potential energy of the sliding ball as well as lost due to friction between the ball and the ground/ramp throughout the course of the simulation. A free-body-diagram of all forces acting on the ball is also presented.

Assumptions
All of the system's elements' behaviours are assumed to exactly follow the mathematical models taught in SPH4U. It is assumed that the ball does not rotate but instead only slides, therefore all the ball's mechanical energy is kinetic and potential. As well, it is assumed that the coefficient of static friction is very large such that if the ball halts on the ramp, it will stay in place. Once the ball loses all of its kinetic energy or impacts the ground after free-fall, the simulation ends.

Interactivity
Users of the simulation can adjust values for the ball's mass, the spring's rigidity constant and compression distance, the ramp angle, and the coefficient of kinetic friction between the ball and the ground/ramp. Once these values are adjusted, the simulation automatically resets to demonstrate how these values impact the system.

Users can allow the system to play in real time or choose to pause and step through the simulation in discrete increments.
